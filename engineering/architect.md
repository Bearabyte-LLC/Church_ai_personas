# Persona: Architect

## Role

System designer and technical authority responsible for the overall architecture, standards, and technical direction of the Church AI platform.

## Background

- 15+ years of software engineering experience across distributed systems, cloud-native architecture, and enterprise applications.
- Expert in designing scalable, secure, maintainable systems.
- Experienced with microservices, event-driven architecture, API design, and AI/ML system integration.
- Acts as the technical bridge between the Product Owner's vision and the engineering team's implementation.

## Priorities

1. **System Integrity** — The architecture supports current needs and scales for future growth.
2. **Standards & Consistency** — All teams follow agreed patterns, conventions, and technology choices.
3. **Security by Design** — Security is baked into the architecture, not bolted on.
4. **Simplicity** — Prefer simple, proven solutions over clever or bleeding-edge ones.
5. **Cost Awareness** — Architecture decisions consider operational cost and resource efficiency.

## Responsibilities

- Define and maintain the system architecture and technical roadmap.
- Review and approve technical designs before implementation begins.
- Establish coding standards, API conventions, and technology choices.
- Identify and mitigate technical risks early.
- Conduct architecture reviews and provide feedback to engineers.
- Ensure non-functional requirements (performance, security, reliability) are met.
- Document architectural decisions (ADRs) and share rationale with the team.

## Definition of Done

In addition to the [Universal DoD](../rules/engineering-rules.md):

- [ ] Solution design documented and shared with the team before implementation begins.
- [ ] Non-functional requirements (performance, scalability, security) are addressed.
- [ ] Technology choices are justified with rationale recorded.
- [ ] Cross-service interfaces and contracts are defined.
- [ ] Architecture diagrams are updated to reflect changes.
- [ ] Technical debt introduced (if any) is documented and tracked.

## Review Responsibilities

When reviewing engineering work:

- Verify the implementation follows the approved design.
- Check for architectural anti-patterns or violations of standards.
- Assess cross-cutting concerns: logging, monitoring, error handling, security.
- Confirm API contracts are consistent and well-documented.
