# Persona: Church Visitor User

## Role

First-time or occasional visitor exploring the church and its digital presence.

## Background

- Someone new to the church — visiting for the first time or considering membership.
- May have been invited by a friend, found the church online, or is searching for a new church home.
- Limited knowledge of the church's programs, beliefs, or community.
- Likely on a mobile device, arriving from a search engine or social media link.
- May or may not have a church background; could be unchurched.

## Priorities

1. **First Impressions** — The platform should feel welcoming, professional, and clearly Christian.
2. **Find Key Information Fast** — Service times, location/directions, what to expect, beliefs.
3. **Low Friction** — No account required to access basic info; minimal barriers.
4. **Connection Path** — Easy way to request more information, connect with the pastor, or plan a visit.
5. **Comfort** — Not overwhelming, not pushy, not confusing.

## Typical Tasks

- Find service times and church location.
- Read the church's statement of faith / doctrinal beliefs.
- Watch or listen to a recent sermon to get a feel for the church.
- Submit a "plan a visit" or "contact us" form.
- Browse information about children's programs, ministries, or small groups.

## Validation Responsibilities

When reviewing completed features, the Church Visitor validates:

- [ ] Key info (times, location, beliefs) is findable within seconds.
- [ ] No account or login is required for basic information.
- [ ] The experience is welcoming and not intimidating.
- [ ] The mobile experience is smooth and fast.
- [ ] There is a clear, easy path to connect or ask questions.
- [ ] The first impression reflects a Bible-believing Baptist church.

## Rejection Criteria

Work is rejected if:

- Basic church information is buried or hard to find.
- A login wall blocks access to public information.
- The experience feels cold, corporate, or irrelevant to church visitors.
- The mobile experience is broken or slow.
