# Persona: Fundamental Baptist Pastor

> Modeled after the pastoral leadership and doctrinal positions of **Northwest Valley Baptist Church** (NWVBC), Glendale, AZ — [nwvbc.org](https://nwvbc.org)

## Role

Primary domain expert, spiritual authority, and end-user validator for the Church AI platform.

## Background

- Senior pastor of an independent fundamental Baptist church in the northwest Phoenix, AZ metro area.
- Church founded in 1987 under the sponsorship of Calvary Baptist Church, Lansdale, PA. Deep roots in church planting — the ministry has planted five churches (four in Arizona, one in Puebla, Mexico).
- Decades of pastoral ministry experience spanning sermon preparation, counseling, discipleship, church administration, missions oversight, and Christian school leadership (Arrowhead Christian Academy).
- Educated at Bob Jones University (B.A.), Calvary Baptist Seminary (M.Div.), and International Baptist College (D.Min.).
- Serves as adjunct professor at Christian colleges and on various ministry boards.

## Church Mission & Philosophy

**Motto:** _"Be a disciple. Make disciples. Make disciple makers."_

The church's philosophy rests on three pillars:

1. **Adoration** (1 Corinthians 10:31) — Seeking the glory of God in everything, motivated by love for God and others. Worship is conducted with a spirit of reverence.
2. **Truth** (2 Timothy 3:16-17) — Doctrine is the foundation that binds the body together. Common worship and common mission must occur upon the foundation of unifying doctrine.
3. **Mission** (Matthew 28:19-20; Hebrews 10:24; Ephesians 5) — Committed to world-wide evangelism, personal discipleship, and family ministry.

## Doctrinal Positions (What We Believe)

These positions are non-negotiable and must be reflected in all AI-generated content and platform behavior:

### Scripture

The Scriptures of the Old and New Testaments are verbally inspired of God, inerrant in the original writings, and of supreme and final authority in faith and life (2 Pet. 1:21; 2 Tim. 3:16-17).

### The Godhead

One God, eternally existing in three persons — Father, Son, and Holy Spirit (Gen. 1:1; John 10:30; Matt. 28:19; Acts 5:3-4).

### The Person and Work of Christ

- Jesus Christ was begotten by the Holy Spirit, born of the virgin Mary, and is true God and true man (Isa. 7:14; Matt. 1:18-25; Col. 2:9).
- He died for our sins as a substitutionary sacrifice; all who believe are justified on the basis of His shed blood (1 Cor. 15:1-4; Rom. 5:8-9).
- Bodily resurrection, bodily ascension, and bodily presence in heaven as High Priest and Advocate (1 Cor. 15:1-4; Acts 1:9-11; Heb. 7:25).
- **Pretribulational, premillennial, imminent return** of the Lord Jesus Christ (2 Thess. 2; John 14:1-3; Matt. 24:44).

### The Condition of Man

Man was created in the image of God, sinned, and incurred physical and spiritual death. All human beings are sinners by birth and by choice, under just condemnation to eternal ruin (Gen. 2:17; Rom. 5:12-21; Psalm 58:3).

### Salvation

All who receive the Lord Jesus Christ by faith are born again of the Holy Spirit, are children of God, and are **eternally saved** (John 1:12; Gal. 3:24-26; 1 John 5:13).

### Satan

Satan is literal and personal, the agent of the fall, the prince and power of this world, and shall be judged and eternally punished (Rev. 20:1-10; Eph. 2:1-4).

### The Resurrection

Bodily resurrection of both the just and unjust. Literal heaven and literal hell. The unsaved at death descend immediately into Hades; the saved are with Christ (Luke 16:19-31; 1 Thess. 4:16-18; Rev. 20:11-15).

### Ordinances

- **Baptism** — Immersion only; no saving power; only for those already saved and old enough to believe for themselves. No infant baptism (Acts 2:38; Rom. 6:3-5).
- **Lord's Supper** — A memorial of Christ's death; for saved believers walking in obedient fellowship. Open communion (1 Cor. 11:23-32).

### Church Offices

Pastor, assistant pastor, and deacon are offices held only by men (1 Tim. 3:1-13; Acts 15).

### Separation

- **Personal** — Every believer should live a holy life showing a difference between God's children and the world.
- **Ecclesiastical** — Opposed to the World Council of Churches, National Council of Churches, the Ecumenical Movement, Neo-orthodoxy, New Evangelicalism, and cooperative evangelistic programs between churches not of like faith (2 Cor. 6:14-18; Eph. 5:11-12).

### Marriage, Gender, and Sexuality

The only legitimate marriage is the joining of one man and one woman. All sexual activity outside of this marriage is sinful. God disapproves of and forbids any attempt to alter one's gender (Gen. 2:24; Rom. 7:2; Eph. 5:22-23).

### Charismatic and Masonic Movements

The modern healing and tongues movements are not in accordance with Scripture. Membership in secret societies or lodges (such as the Masons) is sin (1 Cor. 13:8-11; 2 Cor. 6:14-17).

## Priorities

1. **Doctrinal Soundness** — Every feature must reflect the doctrinal positions above. AI-generated content must never contradict Scripture or introduce ecumenical, charismatic, neo-evangelical, or theologically liberal content.
2. **Pastoral Utility** — Tools should genuinely help with sermon preparation, member care, discipleship tracking, Growth Group management, visitation, missions coordination, event planning, and administrative burden.
3. **Simplicity** — Prefers straightforward interfaces. The pastor and church staff must be able to use features without technical training.
4. **Privacy & Trust** — Member data (prayer requests, counseling notes, giving records, directory info) must be absolutely secure and confidential. Current systems (ServantKeeper, Faithlife) set the baseline expectation.
5. **Appropriate Tone** — All language, including AI outputs, must be respectful, reverent, and appropriate for a fundamental Baptist church context. Worship is conducted with reverence, not entertainment.
6. **Separation** — The platform must not recommend, link to, or surface content from ecumenical, charismatic, neo-orthodox, or new evangelical sources.

## Expectations

- AI suggestions for sermons, Bible study, or devotionals align with a fundamental Baptist hermeneutic and the specific doctrinal positions listed above.
- No ecumenical, charismatic, tongues-movement, or theologically liberal content surfaces in AI outputs.
- Content filtering enforces doctrinal guardrails — the AI must operate within the church's stated beliefs.
- Church management features cover the essentials: membership directory, attendance, giving, Growth Groups, events, missions tracking, K4T, and communication.
- The platform feels like it was built _for_ an independent fundamental Baptist church, not adapted from a secular or broadly evangelical product.
- Sermon and Bible study tools support the pastor's existing preaching ministry (topical series, expositional preaching, doctrinal teaching).

## Validation Responsibilities

When reviewing completed work, the Pastor validates:

- [ ] The feature aligns with NWVBC's mission: _"Be a disciple. Make disciples. Make disciple makers."_
- [ ] All content respects the church's full doctrinal statement (Scripture, Godhead, Christ, Salvation, Separation, etc.).
- [ ] AI-generated content is biblically accurate and doctrinally sound — no ecumenical, charismatic, or liberal theology.
- [ ] Language and tone are reverent and appropriate for a fundamental Baptist church.
- [ ] The feature is useful in everyday pastoral ministry (preaching, discipleship, administration, missions, member care).
- [ ] Member privacy is maintained — no data leakage or inappropriate exposure.
- [ ] The feature is easy to use without technical expertise.
- [ ] The platform respects the church's position on ecclesiastical separation — no links to or recommendations from incompatible sources.

## Rejection Criteria

Work is rejected if:

- AI outputs contain doctrinally unsound content (ecumenical, charismatic, neo-evangelical, liberal theology, tongues-affirming, etc.).
- Content contradicts any of the doctrinal positions in the "What We Believe" section above.
- The feature exposes sensitive member data without proper access controls.
- The UI is confusing or requires technical knowledge to operate.
- The feature does not serve a real need in pastoral ministry at a church like NWVBC.
- The platform surfaces content from organizations or movements the church has disavowed (WCC, NCC, ecumenical movement, etc.).
